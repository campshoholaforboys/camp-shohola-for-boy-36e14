Frequently Asked Questions About Watersports

What watersports activities does Camp Shohola for Boys offer, and are there any age restrictions?
Camp Shohola for Boys in Greeley, PA offers a variety of watersports including canoeing, kayaking, sailing, paddleboarding, and swimming. Our waterfront program is designed for boys ages 7–16, and activities are tailored by age and skill level. Younger campers start with basic safety and swimming, while older or more experienced campers can participate in advanced sailing and kayaking challenges.
How much does it cost to enroll my child in the watersports program at Camp Shohola, and what does the fee include?
Watersports are included in the standard camp tuition, which varies depending on session length. For Summer 2024, a full 4-week session is $6,950 and includes all instructional watersports activities, equipment, and safety gear. There are no additional fees for participating in watersports, but optional private lessons or specialty clinics may have a small extra charge.
What qualifications and certifications do your watersports instructors have?
All of our waterfront staff are certified in lifeguarding, CPR, and First Aid, with many also holding American Canoe Association (ACA) or US Sailing certifications. We prioritize safety, so staff receive additional training specific to Lake Greeley’s conditions and our camp’s safety protocols before each season begins.
What is the daily schedule for watersports, and how are campers supervised during these activities?
Watersports are scheduled daily after breakfast and again in the afternoon, weather permitting. Campers can sign up for specific activities or participate as part of their cabin group. All activities are supervised by trained staff at a minimum 1:6 staff-to-camper ratio, with a dedicated lifeguard always present at the lakefront.
What happens if bad weather prevents watersports activities? Is there a refund or alternate plan?
Safety is our top priority. If weather conditions in Greeley (such as thunderstorms or high winds) make watersports unsafe, we substitute with indoor or alternative outdoor activities. While we do not issue refunds for missed watersports due to weather, we make every effort to reschedule activities so campers get a full experience during their stay.
