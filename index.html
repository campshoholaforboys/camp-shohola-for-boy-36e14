Frequently Asked Questions About Land Sports

What is included in the land sports program at Camp Shohola for Boys?
Our land sports program in Greeley, PA includes a wide variety of activities such as soccer, baseball, basketball, tennis, lacrosse, archery, and ultimate frisbee. Each camper gets to choose their preferred activities during orientation, and sessions are led by experienced coaches. We focus on skill development, teamwork, and sportsmanship, ensuring boys of all ability levels have a positive experience.
How much does it cost to enroll my child in the summer camp's land sports program?
For Summer 2024, our standard session (4 weeks) is $6,900 per camper, which covers all activities, including land sports, meals, lodging, and supplies. There are no extra fees for participating in land sports. We offer early registration discounts and limited sibling discounts. Please contact our office to discuss payment plans or to request our full rate sheet.
Is Camp Shohola licensed and accredited for operating youth sports programs?
Yes, Camp Shohola is fully licensed by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania and complies with all state and Pike County health and safety regulations. We are also accredited by the American Camp Association (ACA), which means our land sports facilities, staff qualifications, and safety protocols meet rigorous national standards.
What is the registration process, and when should I sign up for the land sports camp?
Registration opens in September for the following summer and typically fills up by early spring. We recommend signing up as soon as possible to secure your spot, especially if your child is interested in specific land sports. The process involves completing an online application, submitting medical forms, and placing a deposit. Once your registration is confirmed, we’ll send a welcome packet with packing lists and activity selection forms.
What guarantees does Camp Shohola offer regarding supervision and safety during land sports activities?
Camper safety is our top priority. All land sports sessions are supervised by trained counselors and certified coaches, with a camper-to-staff ratio of 6:1 or better. First aid and emergency protocols are in place, and staff are CPR and First Aid certified. Our equipment is inspected regularly, and we provide protective gear for all contact sports. Parents receive daily updates through our camp portal, and our staff are always available to address any concerns.
