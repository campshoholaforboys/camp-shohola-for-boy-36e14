Frequently Asked Questions About Commtech

What commtech services does Camp Shohola For Boys offer to campers, and how do they enhance the camp experience?
At Camp Shohola, we provide a range of commtech (communications technology) activities tailored for boys ages 7-16, including radio broadcasting, digital photography, video production, and basic electronics. These programs are designed to spark creativity and teach practical skills in a fun, hands-on environment, all while fitting into our daily camp schedule in Greeley, PA. Our instructors use age-appropriate equipment and ensure projects are collaborative and engaging, helping campers develop teamwork as well as tech skills.
How much does it cost for my child to participate in the commtech program at Camp Shohola?
The commtech program is included in the overall camp tuition—there are no separate or hidden fees for participation. Our 2024 tuition for a four-week session is $6,550, which covers all activities, instruction, lodging, meals, and standard materials. Some advanced projects may require optional materials, but these are rare and always communicated in advance. We strive to provide all necessary equipment so every camper can fully participate.
How is camper safety and privacy handled when using technology during commtech activities?
Safety and privacy are top priorities at Camp Shohola. All technology use is closely supervised by our trained staff. Campers do not have unsupervised internet access, and all online activity is limited to pre-approved, secure platforms for educational purposes only. Any media created (photos, videos, broadcasts) is reviewed by staff before sharing, and we follow strict guidelines to protect campers’ identities and personal information. Parents are informed about all commtech activities and can opt out if desired.
Are there any special permits or licensing required for the commtech programs, like radio broadcasting, and are these in place at your Greeley, PA location?
Yes, for our radio broadcasting activities, we operate under a Low Power FM (LPFM) radio license, compliant with FCC regulations for educational, non-commercial use. All equipment and transmissions at our Greeley, PA camp are regularly inspected and maintained to meet legal requirements. Our staff receive annual training on compliance and safety to ensure we remain up to date with all necessary permits and guidelines.
What happens if my child wants to switch out of the commtech program once camp has started, and are there any guarantees about their experience?
We want every camper to enjoy their summer, so we offer flexible scheduling: if your child decides the commtech program isn’t the right fit, they can switch to another activity—like sports, arts, or nature exploration—at any time. While we can’t guarantee a specific outcome, we do guarantee that our staff will work personally with your child to find activities they love and feel comfortable in. Our goal is to make sure each camper has a rewarding and memorable summer at Camp Shohola.
